Aymen’s Mission

The Challenge

Mental health care lacks a cohesive framework. Without a shared understanding of human well-being, treatments vary widely, cost a lot and are generally not efficient - successful treatment rates vary between 30-40%. A unified and coherent theory is essential to guide effective interventions.

How can we help people if we don’t agree: 1) on what the roots of the problems are and 2) on what helps them?

The Opportunity

Aymen’s passion for psychology and philosophy opened up his possibility to study at Oxford. The one-year philosophy program he was accepted to, will equip him with the tools to contribute to the unification of theories within psychology, bridging gaps in modern psychotherapy.

The Plan

After Oxford, Aymen aims to continue in this endeavor. He thinks that a coherent and comprehensive theory will ultimately increase treatment efficacy, and potentially reduce research costs. The funds raised (€36,800 for tuition) will support his studies, supplemented by scholarships and savings (£7,730).

Aymen’s Background

Aymen, a 24-year-old psychology student with a philosophical bent, has built a strong foundation for his Oxford journey. Aymen is currently completing his bachelor’s degree in psychology at the University of Amsterdam, whose Faculty of Behavioral Sciences ranks in the global top 10 (QS) and emphasizes experimental and clinical psychology. His studies have revealed the field’s deep theoretical fragmentation—more akin to an explosion in a confetti factory than a coherent science.

Alongside his academic work, he is active in the student wing of the Netherlands Institute for Psychologists, co-organizing lectures and conferences for over 300 students seeking to engage beyond the curriculum and connect with leaders in the field. Before Amsterdam, he completed a year-long philosophy program at the College of Anton Neuwirth, where he wrote approximately 50 essays under a rigorous academic team. It was there he came to understand that every practice rests on a theory—and that the quality of practice depends on the clarity and strength of that theory. This insight continues to guide his academic path.
